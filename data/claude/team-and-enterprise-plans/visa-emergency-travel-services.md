# Visa Emergency Services for International Travelers

## Lost or Stolen Card — Immediate Steps

If your Visa card is lost or stolen while traveling internationally:

### Step 1: Block Your Card
Call Visa's Global Emergency line: **+1 303 967 1090** (available 24/7)

Within India: **000-800-100-1219** (toll-free)

Your card will be blocked within approximately 30 minutes of being reported.

### Step 2: Emergency Cash Advance
Visa can arrange an emergency cash advance of up to **$10,000** at the nearest Visa member bank. Requirements:
- Valid government-issued photo ID
- Knowledge of your card number or the last 4 digits
- The name of your issuing bank

### Step 3: Replacement Card
An emergency replacement card can be delivered within:
- **1-3 business days** in major cities
- **3-5 business days** in other locations
- Express delivery available for an additional fee

### ATM Card Retention

If an ATM retains your card:
1. Contact your issuing bank immediately
2. Note the ATM location, bank name, and time
3. Request a temporary card from any Visa member bank
4. File a card retrieval request — the ATM operator has 5 business days to return or destroy the card

### Zero Liability Protection

Visa's Zero Liability policy means you're not held responsible for unauthorized transactions made with your account or account information. This applies to:
- Purchases made in-store, online, or via phone
- ATM transactions (when reported promptly)
- Contactless/tap-to-pay transactions

Note: Zero Liability does not apply to:
- Transactions authorized with your PIN where you shared your PIN with the unauthorized user
- Commercial/business card programs (varies by issuer)
- ATM transactions in certain regions

### Travel Notifications

To avoid your card being blocked for suspicious activity:
1. Set up travel notifications through your bank's app
2. Carry a backup payment method
3. Keep Visa's emergency number saved in your phone contacts
