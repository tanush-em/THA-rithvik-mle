# Visa Minimum Transaction Amounts — Consumer Rights

## US Federal Law

Under **Section 920(a)(2)(B) of the Electronic Fund Transfer Act** (as amended by the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform Act, 2010), merchants in the United States are permitted to set a **minimum purchase amount of up to $10** for credit card transactions.

### Key Points

- **Maximum minimum**: Merchants cannot set a minimum higher than $10 for Visa credit card transactions
- **Debit cards excluded**: This rule applies only to credit cards. Merchants cannot set minimums for Visa debit card transactions
- **Government payments**: No minimums allowed for government-related payments
- **US territories**: The $10 maximum minimum applies in all US states and territories, including Puerto Rico, Guam, US Virgin Islands, American Samoa, and the Northern Mariana Islands

### What This Means for Consumers

If a merchant in the US sets a minimum purchase of $10 or less:
- This is **legal** and Visa's network rules **allow it**
- You cannot force the merchant to process a smaller transaction
- The merchant should clearly post the minimum amount at the point of sale

If a merchant sets a minimum **above $10**:
- This **violates** both federal law and Visa's merchant agreement
- You can report the merchant to Visa through visa.com/merchant-violations
- You can also report to your state's attorney general

### International Rules

Outside the United States, minimum transaction requirements vary by country:

| Region | Rule |
|---|---|
| European Union | No minimums allowed for consumer credit/debit |
| United Kingdom | No minimums allowed (post-Brexit maintained) |
| Canada | Minimums allowed up to CAD $10 (credit only) |
| Australia | No minimums allowed |
| India | No specific federal regulation; varies by merchant agreement |
| Rest of World | Varies by local law and Visa's regional operating regulations |

## Visa's Network Rules

Visa's Core Rules and Visa Product and Service Rules state:

> A Merchant must not establish a minimum or maximum Transaction amount as a condition for honoring a Visa Card **unless permitted by applicable law**.

In jurisdictions where minimums are permitted by law, Visa allows merchants to implement them within the legal limits.

## Surcharges vs. Minimums

These are different concepts:
- **Minimum**: A minimum purchase amount to use a card (allowed in some jurisdictions)
- **Surcharge**: An extra fee added for using a card (allowed in some jurisdictions, but not all)
- **Convenience fee**: A fee for using an alternative payment channel (e.g., paying by phone)

## What to Do if You Encounter a Problem

1. Ask the merchant to show their posted minimum/surcharge policy
2. If you believe the merchant is violating the rules, pay with cash and note the merchant name and location
3. Report to Visa: Call the number on the back of your card
4. You can also contact your issuing bank, which can file a merchant violation report with Visa
